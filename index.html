World Population

Introduction

In demographics, the world population is the number of humans currently living, and was estimated to have reached 7,800,000,000 people as of March 2020. It took over 2 million years of human prehistory and history for the world's population to reach 1 billion and only 200 years more to grow to 7 billion. [1]

Population of Countries throughout Year 1955 - 2020

The map below shows the population of each country from year 1955 to 2020. Based on the map, we see that China and India maintained to have the highest population over the years compared to the other countries.

Top 10 Density Countries

Population density is a measurement of population per unit area. In simple terms, population density refers to the number of people living in an area per square kilometre. [2]

The bar chart to the left shows the top 10 countries with the highest density. Though Monaco has a lower population but it has a small land area.

Top 10 Highest Country Population Percentage in Year 2020

Population percentage is the population of a country over world population.

The pie chart to the right shows the top 10 countries with the highest population percentage in year 2020. China can be seen to have the highest percentage due to its high population.

The 'Country' legend on the side can be used to highlight the selected country.

Countries Fertility Rate & Death Rate (2020)

Fertility and death rate are the few factors that affects the population of a country. Fertility rate is the average number of children born to women during their reproductive years. For the population in a given area to remain stable, an overall total fertility rate of 2.1 is needed, assuming no immigration or emigration occurs. [3] Meanwhile, mortality rate, or death rate, is a measure of the number of deaths (in general, or due to a specific cause) in a particular population, scaled to the size of that population, per unit of time. [4]

The bubble plot below shows the fertility and death of each country in year 2020.
